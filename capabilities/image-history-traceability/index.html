Live investigation library

See what the machine is rejecting without standing at the machine.

The reject bin tells operators that something was removed. It does not always tell FSQA what the concern looked like, whether similar events are repeating, or whether the pattern needs follow-up.

GreyscaleAI gives authorized users a cloud-accessible image library so teams can review inspection events in near real time, away from the production floor. A QA manager, food-safety lead, plant manager, or technical support user can open the record, inspect the image, review the context, and decide whether the issue needs escalation, reinspection, supplier follow-up, or deeper analysis.

Retention and review

Keep the evidence that matters.

Food-safety questions rarely come at a convenient time. A customer complaint, audit request, foreign-material review, or repeat reject pattern can require teams to reconstruct what happened hours, days, or weeks later.

GreyscaleAI stores inspection images, event metadata, and machine context according to customer-defined retention policies. Teams can search by time, SKU, line, event type, machine, lot, and related metadata to support investigations, traceability requests, and recurring quality review.

01

Retention by policy

Image and event retention is configured with the customer rather than assumed as a fixed generic setting.

02

Searchable metadata

Time, product, line, event type, lot, and machine details help teams narrow the record quickly.

03

Controlled access

Authorized users can review, export, and manage records according to approved access and retention workflows.

Governed evidence

Controlled records reduce risk better than scattered records.

Some teams worry that image history creates liability. The real risk is usually less obvious: incomplete paper logs, screenshots saved outside the process, handwritten notes that are hard to read, and records that are difficult to find when a serious question comes up.

GreyscaleAI is designed to make inspection evidence governed and useful. The goal is not to keep more data for its own sake. The goal is to keep the right inspection evidence, under the right retention policy, so QA and food-safety teams can answer hard questions quickly.

  • Policy-controlled retention
    Records are retained according to the customer’s deployment and QA requirements.
  • Permissioned review
    Access can be limited to appropriate QA, operations, technical, and management users.
  • Exportable evidence
    Teams can pull supporting records for internal review, customer reporting, audit support, and corrective-action documentation.

HACCP challenge records

Hands-free challenge documentation.

Routine system challenges are essential, but the workflow is often manual. An operator runs a challenge, interacts with the machine interface, writes down the result, and someone later has to prove when it happened and what the system saw.

GreyscaleAI supports proprietary challenge cards that allow authorized teams to challenge the inspection system without navigating the operator interface during production. When a challenge event occurs, the system can capture the inspection image, timestamp, product context, line, machine, and result as part of the image history.

QA teams can search and export challenge records for verification, internal review, customer reporting, and audit support. Where aligned with the customer’s approved QA and HACCP procedures, this can reduce handwritten challenge logs and make reporting easier.

1

Run the challenge

An authorized team member runs the routine challenge using GreyscaleAI challenge cards, without stopping to operate the UI.

2

Capture the record

The system captures the image, timestamp, line, product context, machine context, and result.

3

Review and report

QA can search, review, and export challenge records by date, line, SKU, machine, or audit period.

Search interface

Find the right event before the question escalates.

Image history is only useful if teams can find the right record quickly. GreyscaleAI brings the inspection image, AI decision, machine metadata, and production context into one review workflow.

From image history to Insights

Start with the image. Then look beyond the reject.

Image history helps teams answer, “What did the machine reject?” Insights helps teams answer the next questions: Is this happening repeatedly? Is it tied to a product, shift, supplier, machine state, or process change? Are there elevated-risk windows around the reject that deserve review?

When a single event raises a larger question, teams can use GreyscaleAI Insights to move from image review into reject trends, machine visibility, alerts, and Beyond Rejects workflows.

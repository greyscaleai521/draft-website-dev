Capability

Checkweighing & Grading

X-ray images can be used to estimate relative mass, support checkweighing decisions, and sort product into quality bands when the application is validated for it.

How it works

Weight and density are derived from the image, not guessed from the package label.

GreyscaleAI models use image intensity, detected product area, and product-specific calibration to estimate weight-related and density-related signals. The useful accuracy range depends on product consistency, presentation, packaging, and line conditions.

  • Derived from X-ray data
    Mass and density signals come from what the system sees in the inspection image.
  • Application calibration
    Models are tuned to the specific product and package format so the output is meaningful for that line.
  • Used for decisions
    Estimated mass and related quality signals can feed diversion, grading, and operator review workflows.

Typical expectations

Accuracy ranges vary by product, but teams still need practical targets.

Uniform packaged items

Tighter range

Best fit for applications where the product geometry is controlled and the image is consistent.

Natural protein

Broader range

Variable density and shape usually widen the expected tolerance window.

Quality bands

Relative sorting

Grading often matters more than exact absolute weight when sorting by visualized quality.

Validation

Line-specific

Expected accuracy should be reviewed against your actual product and packaging before committing to thresholds.

Grading workflow

Sort product into bands that operations can actually use.

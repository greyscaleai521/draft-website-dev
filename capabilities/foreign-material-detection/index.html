Foreign Material Detection

High-resolution x-ray inspection helps food producers detect metal, bone, glass, stones, and some dense plastics while preserving image evidence for review.

Talk Through Your Product See Platform Overview

What teams need

More than a binary reject.

Food safety teams need to confirm what the machine saw, how the event was classified, and whether the product and package create blind spots. GreyscaleAI combines inspection images, AI review, and event history so decisions can move faster.

  • Detectable risks
    Common targets include metal, calcified bone, glass, stones, and dense plastics where contrast against the product is sufficient.
  • Application caveats
    Performance depends on product thickness, moisture, density, orientation, packaging format, contaminant size, and line speed.
  • Review workflow
    Rejected units can be traced back to the underlying X-ray image and machine context for QA investigation.

Detection matrix

Detection depends on product and packaging, not just contaminant type.

This matrix is directional, not a blanket guarantee. Validation should always be done with your actual product, package, contaminant set, and line conditions.

Metal

High contrast

Often among the easiest contaminants to detect when product density is moderate.

Bone

Product-specific

Detection varies widely with product thickness, natural density, and bone composition.

Glass & stone

Dense targets

Usually strong candidates where the product does not mask the contaminant.

Dense plastic

Case by case

Some dense plastics can be detected, but contrast is usually lower than metal or glass.

Example X-ray views

Image evidence helps teams confirm what happened.

Metal fragment in a formed product

A compact dense object stands out clearly against the product body, making the event faster to verify.

Bone risk in a tray-packed item

Bone detection depends heavily on natural product density and package geometry, so application testing matters.

Dense inclusion in linked product

For irregular products, image review helps determine whether the event is a true contaminant or a benign density change.

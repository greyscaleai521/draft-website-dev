Product Quality Inspection

The same X-ray images used for inspection can also surface quality signals such as voids, clumps, missing components, density variation, and broken pieces.

Quality signals

See product conditions that operators and QA teams often miss at line speed.

GreyscaleAI uses high-resolution images and AI models to classify product-specific conditions that relate to fill, distribution, structure, count, and breakage. The useful signals vary by product, package, and inspection objective.

  • Voids and underfill
    Spot missing product mass or empty spaces inside formed items.
  • Clumps and distribution
    Detect uneven ingredient spread or density pockets inside packaged food.
  • Missing components
    Count expected pieces or confirm that required inclusions are present.
  • Broken product
    Flag fractures, collapsed structure, or shape loss that matters to quality grading.

Example image conditions

Images give teams a shared view of what changed.

Internal void

Low-density gaps can indicate underfill or structural inconsistency that is not obvious from the outside.

Missing component

Count-based models help confirm whether an expected portion or inclusion is absent.

Density variation

Broader regions of changed density can point to clumping, settling, or other process-related quality drift.

How teams use it

Quality inspection becomes more useful when it is tied to action.

Operator feedback

Show the line team when a condition starts to drift instead of waiting for downstream complaints or manual audits.

Grading inputs

Turn image-derived quality signals into quality bands or diversion logic where the application supports it.

Trend review

Use Insights to compare conditions by SKU, shift, supplier, or line so recurring issues are easier to isolate.

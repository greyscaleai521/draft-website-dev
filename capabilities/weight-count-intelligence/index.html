Weight & Count Intelligence

GreyscaleAI turns inspection images, checkweigher reject signals, and product-specific count checks into operational insight, helping teams see giveaway, drift, and packages that deserve review before small changes become expensive.

Discuss your line See Insights

How it works

See when production starts running heavy.

A dedicated checkweigher is the right tool for precise package-weight control. GreyscaleAI adds the broader operating picture by trending image-derived weight estimates, checkweigher reject signals, and inspection context across products, lots, shifts, lines, and facilities.

  • Weight-estimate trends
    Image-derived weight signals help show whether production is centered, drifting, or consistently over target.
  • Checkweigher signal capture
    Checkweigher rejects can be brought into Insights so teams can review weight events alongside inspection data.
  • Process context
    Weight-related signals can be paired with count or expected-product checks where the product presentation supports it.

Insights for giveaway and drift

See giveaway before it becomes margin loss.

When a line drifts heavy, every acceptable package can quietly include product giveaway. GreyscaleAI helps teams see how much overfill is happening, when it started, and where to investigate before the cost compounds.

Operational outcomes

Turn weight data into margin protection.

Giveaway monitoring

Track estimated overfill by product, lot, shift, line, and facility so teams can see where margin is being lost in normal production.

Weight plus count context

Weight is one signal. Count and expected-product checks add package-composition context where the product presentation supports it.

Proactive alerts

Turn gradual drift into alerts, so operators and quality teams can adjust before giveaway, variation, or review events increase.

Designed to complement checkweighers

Keep the precision tool. Add the operating intelligence.

Checkweighers remain the standard for precise legal-for-trade weight control. GreyscaleAI is not positioned as a replacement for that role. Instead, GreyscaleAI brings weight-related signals, checkweigher rejects, inspection results, and product-specific context into Insights so teams can understand what is changing on the line.

Passing weight is not the same as controlled production. A package can pass a minimum-weight requirement while the process is still drifting heavy, giving away product, or creating packages that deserve count or presence review.

Monitoring examples

Signals that help teams act earlier.

  • Average estimated weight
    Review by product, lot, shift, line, and facility.
  • Overfill trends
    Find patterns that point to giveaway.
  • Variation
    Spot signals of upstream process instability.
  • Checkweigher rejects
    Review alongside image-derived inspection data.
  • Outlier packages
    Surface packages that deserve review.
  • Count or expected-product signals
    Monitor package-composition checks where validated.
  • Drift alerts
    Alert teams when the process begins drifting heavy.

Weight plus count

Weight does not always tell the whole package story.

Total mass can be acceptable while a package still deserves review for count, expected product presence, or distribution. GreyscaleAI connects weight-related signals with image-based count and presence checks where validated, giving teams a broader view of whether production is both on-weight and complete.

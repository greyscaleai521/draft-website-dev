Model comparison

Compare HRX inspection systems side by side.

Start with aperture, cabinet type, conveyor format, line speed, and product fit. Final system selection should still be reviewed against your actual product dimensions, package format, line speed, sanitation requirements, and inspection goals.

Published specs are from the January 2026 HRX brochures. Maximum line speed and detection performance are product-dependent and should be validated for the application.

Larger products and broader packages

Start with HRX660, HRX660AQ, or HRX508AQ when the application needs a wider inspection opening or a 60 x 30 in conveyor format.

Washdown environments

Use the AQ models when the application calls for a published IP69K cabinet configuration: HRX660AQ, HRX508AQ, or HRX380AQ.

Compact lines and smaller formats

Start with HRX380 or HRX380AQ for compact line-side inspection, smaller packages, cheese, packaged goods, and component verification workflows.

How to choose

Start with the product and line. Then narrow the HRX configuration.

The comparison table shows the published differences between models. Use these seven inputs to define the physical fit before treating any brochure specification as a recommendation.

01–02

Product and package

01

Product size and orientation

Record the maximum product width, height, and length, plus the orientation that will enter the system. Use the largest expected format, not the average case.

02

Package type and materials

Identify whether the product is loose, bulk, bagged, boxed, tray-packed, wrapped, or another format. Note package features such as clips, foil, folds, and dense components for application review.

03–05

Inspection and throughput

03

Inspection goal

State the production question the system must answer: foreign material, bone, product or package quality, count or presence, weight intelligence, or a defined combination. Final fit should be based on a product-specific review, not a generic detection claim.

04

Line speed and product spacing

Provide normal and maximum line speed, throughput, product spacing, and whether products touch or overlap. Published maximum speed is a machine specification; usable inspection performance remains product- and application-dependent.

05

Aperture and conveyor fit

Confirm the required inspection opening, usable belt width, product transfer, conveyor height, and reject arrangement. Include clearance for normal product and package variation.

06–07

Installation and environment

06

Available space and integration

Measure the available footprint and identify infeed, outfeed, conveyor height, guarding, controls, reject confirmation, and access needed for cleaning and maintenance.

07

Washdown and operating environment

Document the sanitation method, washdown intensity, temperature, humidity, dust, and plant standards. Use these requirements to determine which cabinet and protection level should be reviewed.
